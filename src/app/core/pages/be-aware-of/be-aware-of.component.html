  3. {{"Real estate tax certificate if the property is old" | translate}}

{{'Second: Terms and Conditions' | translate}}
  1. {{"Full name of the buyer and seller - ID number (preferably each party obtains a copy of the other's ID) - Full residential address" | translate}}
  2. {{"Detailed description of the property being sold (whether it's an apartment, vacant land, shop, or complete property), with precise area measurement (the contract will be rejected by the Real Estate Authority in case of any shortage or excess in the area written in the contract, exceeding 10%)" | translate }}
  3. {{"Full details of the sale, according to the property number in the neighborhood, street name, and region, as well as the address of the property and the province, then a statement about the plot of land, including the plot number, square, and basin, based on the spatial data" | translate}}
  4. {{"Clear statement of the price and the payment method." | translate}}
  5. {{"Explanation of how the seller owns the property or the apartment being sold, including all consecutive contracts for the property (in case of heirs, mention the number and date of declaring inheritance to reach the number of the registered contract and the court that issued it). In addition, the buyer must receive all previous ownership contracts from the seller (in case of purchasing an apartment or a shop from the owner of a building or tower and due to the difficulty of delivering those documents, as the owner sells to others, copies of all sales contracts for the land of the property must be obtained from the owner, and the owner must sign each page of the copies indicating that they are true copies of the contract)." | translate}}
  6. {{"Mention the building permit number and date in case of selling apartments and shops." | translate}}
  7. {{"There must be an explicit clause that the owner guarantees hidden defects fully, and acknowledges responsibility in case of any hidden defect in the property, whether it is material or legal. This is in addition to the other usual clauses of guaranteeing the absence of any financial obligations on the property to the state, such as taxes, water, or electricity, prior to the date of the sales contract." | translate}}

{{"Second: Terms and Conditions" | translate}}
  1. {{"The unregistered sales contract is considered a valid contract, and it does not prevent the sale from being valid." | translate}}
  2. {{"Both registered and unregistered contracts are considered valid." | translate}}
  3. {{"The buyer has the right to demand delivery of the property and request it to be placed under custody if there are concerns about the property's safety." | translate}}
  4. {{"The buyer has legal ownership of the property and can file lawsuits related to it, including eviction of the seller from the property and requesting its delivery to the buyer." | translate}}
  5. {{"The buyer has the right to object to the sale of the property to a tenant by the seller before registering the contract, and the seller cannot lease the property to the buyer despite being the owner until the registration is complete. However, if the seller has not yet delivered the property to the tenant, the buyer can request its delivery." | translate}}
  6. {{"The buyer has ownership rights to any crops or fruits produced by the property." | translate}}
  7. {{"In the case of an unregistered contract, the buyer has the right to demand the seller to deliver any crops produced by the property." | translate}}
  8. {{"The buyer has ownership rights to the registered property, while the unregistered contract transfers all rights and lawsuits related to the sale to the buyer. The buyer can receive the property and evict any intruders, but ownership rights are not transferred." | translate}}
  9. {{"Before registration, the buyer is only a regular debtor to the seller and has no direct relationship with the tenant. The buyer's relationship with the seller ends when the registration is complete." | translate}}
  10. {{"If the seller (old owner) transfers the lease contract to the buyer (new owner) before the registration, the buyer can demand the tenant's rights, including rent, without the need for the old owner's involvement." | translate}}
  11. {{"The buyer has the right to benefit from the property and all rights related to it, and can replace the seller in these rights before other partners in the property. This includes the right to enjoy what the seller had before the sale, and to benefit from it in proportion to the buyer's share in the property." | translate}}
  12. {{"The deposit is the amount that one of the contractors provides to the other when creating the contract, and the contractors may agree to make their contract binding and final or allow each of them the right to sign or cancel the contract. This is determined solely by the intention of the contractors or the value of the compensation agreed upon in case of contract termination due to one of the contractors' failure to fulfill their obligations or the value of the deposit. The deposit may be the amount paid by the buyer to the seller under the sales contract, which the buyer will lose if the sale is not completed, or it may be part of the price that is not subject to the seller's jurisdiction as compensation if the buyer cancels the contract due to their mistake and causes harm to the seller. The buyer's cancellation may be due to a hidden defect in the property sold." | translate}}
  13. {{"If the sales contract includes a condition in which the seller prohibits the buyer from disposing of the property until the seller's death, and the seller retains the right to benefit from it throughout their life as a guarantee of their right, this does not prevent the favourite from being considered a valid sale contract for ownership. This condition does not mean that the sale is only a will." | translate}}
  14. {{"The seller may issue a sales contract in the form of a gift contract for his children, and it is considered a valid contract as long as the favourite is not subject to any additional conditions after the seller's death. If the contract is executed during the seller's lifetime, who has waived his rights to the property under this contract, and the contract is in the possession of the administrator who has taken the necessary registration procedures or submitted it to the court to obtain the validity of the seller's signature, then the contract is valid and effective." | translate}}

{{"Required documents, conditions, and provisions for buying a piece of land:" | translate}}
{{"First: Required Documents" | translate}}
  1. {{"The buyer must verify the seller's identity, description, and whether they are an authorized representative or agent of a company by reviewing official documents and keeping a copy." | translate}}
  2. {{"The buyer must confirm the validity and duration of the authorization by sending a recent official copy of it." | translate}}
  3. {{"The buyer must review and confirm the validity of the seller's property title by obtaining a recent official copy." | translate}}
  4. {{"The buyer must obtain a negative certificate regarding any property favourites related to the land." | translate}}
  5. {{"The buyer should seek assistance from the survey and real estate registration departments to obtain an approved land division." | translate}}
  6. {{"The buyer should consult the engineering departments in the neighborhoods or city councils to confirm whether the land is suitable for construction or not." | translate}}
  7. {{"The buyer should avoid purchasing land owned by government entities such as housing ministry or city agencies." | translate}}
  8. {{"If the seller's property title is registered with the real estate registry or the land register, the buyer must check the ownership sequence of the seller until the last registered contract. If the ownership is not registered, the buyer should investigate the possibility of registering it. There is a risk of invalidating any contract in the ownership sequence, including the seller's contract, if it is not registered." | translate}}
