Required Documents, Terms, and Conditions for Buying an Apartment:
First: Required Documents
  • Title deed
  • Preliminary sales contract between the parties
  • Real estate tax certificate if the property is old
  • Building permit if the property is new, and there are procedures to be followed through the Real Estate Authority, such as issuing a determination statement or a change form, and paying the fees, etc.
Second: Terms and Conditions
  • Full name of the buyer and seller - ID number (preferably each party obtains a copy of the other's ID) - Full residential address
  • Detailed description of the property being sold (whether it's an apartment, vacant land, shop, or complete property), with precise area measurement (the contract will be rejected by the Real Estate Authority in case of any shortage or excess in the area written in the contract, exceeding 10%)
  • Full details of the sale, according to the property number in the neighborhood, street name, and region, as well as the address of the property and the province, then a statement about the plot of land, including the plot number, square, and basin, based on the spatial data.
  • Clear statement of the price and the payment method.
  • Explanation of how the seller owns the property or the apartment being sold, including all consecutive contracts for the property (in case of heirs, mention the number and date of declaring inheritance to reach the number of the registered contract and the court that issued it). In addition, the buyer must receive all previous ownership contracts from the seller (in case of purchasing an apartment or a shop from the owner of a building or tower and due to the difficulty of delivering those documents, as the owner sells to others, copies of all sales contracts for the land of the property must be obtained from the owner, and the owner must sign each page of the copies indicating that they are true copies of the contract).
  • Mention the building permit number and date in case of selling apartments and shops.
  • There must be an explicit clause that the owner guarantees hidden defects fully, and acknowledges responsibility in case of any hidden defect in the property, whether it is material or legal. This is in addition to the other usual clauses of guaranteeing the absence of any financial obligations on the property to the state, such as taxes, water, or electricity, prior to the date of the sales contract.
